### H2: What Is Canada's eTA System?
**Electronic Travel Authorization for Air Travelers**

**Overview:**
- Pre-screening system for visa-exempt foreign nationals
- Required for flying to or transiting through Canada by air
- NOT required for land or sea entry
- NOT required for US citizens

**Key Details:**
- **Cost:** CAD $7 (approximately USD $5)
- **Validity:** 5 years or until passport expires (whichever comes first)
- **Multiple Entries:** Unlimited entries during validity period
- **Processing Time:** Typically approved within minutes, can take up to 72 hours
- **Application:** Online only at official Canada.ca website

**Who Needs eTA:**
- Visa-exempt foreign nationals flying to Canada
- Transiting through Canadian airports to another country
- Example: UK citizen flying from London to Toronto needs eTA
- Example: Australian citizen connecting through Vancouver needs eTA

**Who Does NOT Need eTA:**
- US citizens (only need valid passport)
- Canadian citizens and permanent residents
- Travelers entering by land (car, bus, train)
- Travelers entering by sea (cruise ship, ferry)
- Travelers with valid Canadian visas

**Application Process:**
1. Apply online at canada.ca/eta
2. Have passport, email address, and credit card ready
3. Answer security and eligibility questions
4. Pay CAD $7 fee
5. Receive approval (usually within minutes)
6. eTA electronically linked to passport

**Important Notes:**
- eTA doesn't guarantee entry (border officer makes final decision)
- Carry proof of eTA confirmation though not always required
- Valid eTA remains linked to same passport only
- New passport requires new eTA application

### H2: Required Documents at Entry
**Essential Entry Requirements:**

1. **Passport Validity**
   - Must be valid for the duration of your stay in Canada
   - Canada doesn't have "6 months rule" like many countries
   - However, passport should be valid beyond planned departure date
   - Recommendation: At least 6 months validity for safety margin

2. **Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA)**
   - Required for visa-exempt nationals arriving by air
   - Must apply before flying to Canada
   - CAD $7 fee, valid for 5 years
   - NOT required for US citizens or land/sea entry

3. **Proof of Onward Travel**
   - Return or onward ticket out of Canada
   - Boarding pass or booking confirmation
   - Travel itinerary showing departure
   - Border officers strictly enforce this requirement

4. **Proof of Sufficient Funds**
   - Bank statements, credit cards, cash, traveler's checks
   - No specific minimum amount published
   - Rule of thumb: CAD $100-$200 per day recommended
   - Employment letter or pay stubs helpful
   - Sponsorship letters with supporting documents

5. **Travel Purpose Documentation**
   - Hotel bookings or accommodation confirmation
   - Invitation letter from Canadian host (if applicable)
   - Conference registration (for business visitors)
   - Return to home country ties (employment, property, family)

6. **Additional Documents (if applicable)**
   - Travel medical insurance (highly recommended but not mandatory)
   - Letter from employer explaining travel purpose
   - Proof of ties to home country
   - Previous travel history

### H2: Length of Stay Rules
**Up to 6 Months Per Visit (Border Officer Determined)**

**Standard Practice:**
- Visa-exempt visitors typically granted up to 6 months
- Exact duration determined by Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) officer at entry
- Based on travel purpose, documentation, and individual circumstances

**Important Details:**
- If no specific date stamped in passport: You can stay 6 months from entry date
- If specific date stamped: You must leave by that date
- Date stamp overrides default 6-month period
- Entry date counts as first day (departure date doesn't count)

**What Border Officers Consider:**
- Purpose of visit (tourism, business, family visit)
- Proof of funds
- Return ticket/travel plans
- Ties to home country (job, property, family)
- Previous travel history
- Duration of requested stay

**Extending Your Stay:**
- Must apply at least 30 days before current status expires
- Apply online to Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)
- Fee: CAD $100
- Processing time: Several weeks to months
- Extensions rarely guaranteed
- Must maintain legal status while waiting for decision

**Multiple Entries:**
- Visa-exempt travelers can make multiple visits
- Each visit typically up to 6 months
- No strict rule on minimum time between visits
- However, spending more time in Canada than home country raises concerns
- Officers may deny entry if they suspect unauthorized residence
- Common pattern: Spending 6 months in Canada, 1 month away, returning = red flag

**Tips:**
- Be honest with border officers about travel plans
- Have all documentation organized and accessible
- Don't overstay previous authorizations
- Keep records of all entry/exit dates
- If uncertain about stay duration, ask border officer for clarification

### H2: Overstay Penalties in Canada
**Serious Immigration Consequences**

**Canada's Penalties:**
1. **Removal Order:** Immediate deportation from Canada at your expense
2. **Future Entry Refusal:** Denial of future entry to Canada
3. **Entry Bans:** 1-5 years or permanent inadmissibility depending on circumstances
4. **Immigration Record:** Recorded in CBSA/IRCC databases permanently
5. **Impact on Other Countries:** Affects visa applications to US, UK, Australia, Schengen, etc.
6. **Loss of eTA/Visa:** Current authorizations canceled, future applications affected
7. **Criminal Charges (Rare):** In cases of fraud or serious violations

**Severity Factors:**
- **Short Overstay (Days/Weeks):** Removal order, likely future entry denial, possible short ban
- **Medium Overstay (Months):** Removal order, 1-2 year entry ban, permanent record
- **Long Overstay (Years):** Potential permanent inadmissibility, severe impact on future applications
- **Working Without Authorization:** Criminal charges possible, permanent ban likely
- **Multiple Overstays:** Permanent inadmissibility, no leniency

**Canada's Exit Controls:**
- Canada tracks exits through information sharing with US
- Exit data collected at air, land, and sea borders
- Overstays automatically detected and recorded
- No "grace period" - even 1-day overstay is violation

**Information Sharing:**
- **US-Canada Entry/Exit System:** Automatic sharing of border crossing data
- **Five Eyes Alliance:** Intelligence sharing with US, UK, Australia, New Zealand
- **Immigration Databases:** Global visa databases share overstay information
- **Impact on Other Applications:**
  - US visa applications require declaration of overstays
  - UK, Australia, Schengen applications affected
  - May require additional screening, documentation, or interviews

**If You've Overstayed:**
- Leave Canada immediately if still present
- DO NOT attempt to re-enter without resolving situation
- Consult Canadian immigration lawyer
- May need to apply for Authorization to Return to Canada (ARC)
- ARC fees: CAD $200-$400 depending on case
- ARC processing: Several months
- Approval not guaranteed

**Avoiding Overstay:**
- Track your authorized stay carefully
- Note entry date and calculate 6-month expiry (or stamped date)
- Set reminders well before departure deadline
- Keep all entry/exit documentation
- If emergency prevents departure, contact IRCC immediately to apply for extension
- Never assume you can "just leave and come back" to reset time

**Border Officer Authority:**
- Officers have wide discretion at ports of entry
- Can deny entry even with valid eTA or visa
- Can impose conditions on entry (shorter stay, specific dates)
- Can interrogate about previous stays and compliance
- Can access full immigration history instantly

**Restoration of Status:**
- If you realize you've overstayed while still in Canada (within 90 days of expiry)
- Can apply to restore visitor status
- Fee: CAD $350 (restoration) + $100 (visitor extension)
- Must provide explanation for overstay
- Approval not guaranteed
- Better to apply before expiry than after

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**6 Canada-Specific FAQs:**

1. **Do US citizens need a visa for Canada?**
   - **No, US citizens do NOT need a visa or eTA** for Canada
   - Only need valid US passport
   - Can enter by air, land, or sea without prior authorization
   - Typically granted 6 months per visit
   - Border officer makes final decision on entry and stay duration
   - US permanent residents (green card holders) need eTA or visa depending on passport nationality
   - Use visa requirement checker for specific situations

2. **Do UK citizens need an eTA for Canada?**
   - **Yes, UK citizens need eTA if flying to Canada**
   - eTA required for all flights to or transiting through Canada
   - **NOT required if entering by land or sea** (car, bus, train, cruise, ferry)
   - eTA costs CAD $7, valid for 5 years
   - Apply online before flying at canada.ca/eta
   - Typically approved within minutes (can take up to 72 hours)
   - Same passport needed for travel as used for eTA application
   - New passport requires new eTA

3. **Can I enter Canada by land without eTA?**
   - **Yes - eTA is NOT required for land or sea entry**
   - eTA only applies to air travel (flights)
   - Visa-exempt nationals can enter by land with valid passport only
   - Examples:
     - Driving from US to Canada: No eTA needed
     - Bus/train from US to Canada: No eTA needed
     - Cruise ship to Canadian port: No eTA needed
     - Ferry crossing: No eTA needed
   - Still need to meet all other entry requirements:
     - Valid passport
     - Proof of funds
     - Return travel plans
     - Border officer approval
   - US citizens never need eTA regardless of entry method

4. **How long can I stay in Canada as a tourist?**
   - **Typically up to 6 months per visit**
   - Exact duration determined by border officer at entry
   - If no date stamped in passport: 6 months from entry date
   - If specific date stamped: Must leave by that date
   - Cannot work or study without proper authorization
   - **Can I extend?** Yes, but must apply at least 30 days before expiry
     - Apply online to IRCC
     - Fee: CAD $100
     - Processing: Several weeks to months
     - Approval not guaranteed
   - **Multiple visits:** Can return, but officers monitor frequency
     - Spending more time in Canada than home country = concern
     - May deny entry if suspecting unauthorized residence
   - **Restoration:** If overstay <90 days, can apply to restore status (CAD $350 + $100)

5. **Can I work remotely in Canada as a tourist?**
   - **COMPLICATED - Generally NO for Canadian work**
   - **Remote work for foreign employer (outside Canada):**
     - Gray area in Canadian law
     - Technically may violate visitor status
     - CBSA discourages this practice
     - If discovered, could result in removal
     - Risk: Border officers can ask about work plans
     - Recommendation: Consult immigration lawyer
   - **Remote work for Canadian employer:**
     - Absolutely NOT allowed under visitor status
     - Requires work permit
     - Violations result in removal and entry bans
   - **Digital Nomad Visas:**
     - Canada doesn't currently have digital nomad visa
     - Consider countries with specific digital nomad programs
   - **Safest Approach:**
     - Take vacation time if possible
     - Apply for work permit if working for Canadian entity
     - Don't volunteer information about remote work to border officers
     - Be honest if directly asked

6. **What happens if I overstay in Canada?**
   - **Serious consequences:**
     - Removal order: Deportation at your expense
     - Entry bans: 1-5 years or permanent inadmissibility
     - Recorded permanently in CBSA/IRCC databases
     - Future entry to Canada denied or heavily scrutinized
     - Current eTA/visa canceled
     - Affects visa applications to other countries (US, UK, Australia, Schengen)
   - **Canada's Exit Tracking:**
     - Automatic exit detection through US-Canada data sharing
     - Air, land, and sea exits tracked
     - Overstays flagged immediately
     - No "grace period" - even 1 day is violation
   - **Severity by Duration:**
     - Short (days/weeks): Removal order, likely future denial
     - Medium (months): 1-2 year ban, permanent record
     - Long (years): Permanent inadmissibility
     - Working illegally: Criminal charges possible, permanent ban
   - **If You've Overstayed:**
     - Leave Canada immediately if still present
     - Consult Canadian immigration lawyer
     - May need Authorization to Return to Canada (ARC): CAD $200-$400, several months processing
     - Don't attempt re-entry without resolving
   - **Restoration of Status:**
     - If overstay <90 days and still in Canada: Apply to restore status (CAD $350 + $100)
     - Provide explanation, approval not guaranteed
