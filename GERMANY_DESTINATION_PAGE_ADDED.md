### H2: Length of Stay Rules (Schengen 90/180 Rule)
**The 90/180-Day Rule Explained**

**Core Rule:** 90 days within any 180-day period

**Key Points:**
- Applies to ALL Schengen countries combined (not per country)
- Rolling 180-day period (not calendar-based)
- Time spent in ANY Schengen country counts toward limit
- Entry and exit days both count as full days

**Schengen Zone Countries (27):**
Austria, Belgium, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Italy, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland

**Calculation Method:**
- On any given day, look back 180 days
- Count total days spent in Schengen Area during those 180 days
- Must not exceed 90 days

**Extensions:**
- Tourist visa extensions rarely granted
- Long-stay visas (>90 days) require separate national visa applications
- No automatic reset by leaving Schengen briefly

### H2: Overstay Penalties in Germany
**Serious Consequences Across Schengen**

**Penalties:**
1. **Fines:** Minimum €500, can exceed €3,000 for long overstays
2. **Deportation:** Immediate removal from Germany/Schengen
3. **Entry Bans:** 1-5 years across entire Schengen zone
4. **Criminal Record:** May face criminal charges for intentional overstays
5. **Future Visa Denials:** Severely impacts future Schengen visa applications

**Schengen Information System (SIS):**
- All overstays recorded in central database
- Shared across all 27 Schengen countries
- Affects future travel to any Schengen country
- May impact visa applications to other countries (US, UK, Canada, Australia)

**Grace Period:** NONE - even 1-day overstay is violation

**Advice:**
- Track days carefully using Schengen calculator
- Exit before 90-day limit
- Keep all entry/exit stamps and boarding passes
- If emergency prevents timely departure, contact German immigration immediately

1. **Do I need a visa to visit Germany?**

   - 62+ countries visa-free (Schengen rules)
   - 30+ countries require Schengen visa
   - EU/EEA/Swiss citizens have unlimited access

2. **How long can I stay in Germany without a visa?**
   - **90 days within any 180-day period** (Schengen rule)
   - Applies across ALL Schengen countries combined, not per country
   - Both entry and exit days count as full days
   - No automatic reset by leaving Germany to another Schengen country
   - Extensions rarely granted without special circumstances

3. **What passport validity does Germany require?**
   - **Minimum 3 months beyond intended departure from Schengen Area**
   - Passport must be issued within last 10 years
   - At least 2 blank pages required
   - Strictly enforced at border
   - Airlines will deny boarding if requirement not met

4. **Does Germany offer e-Visa applications?**
   - **No, Germany does not offer e-Visa** for Schengen tourist visas
   - Traditional visa applications at German consulates or visa centers
   - **ETIAS coming soon:** €7 electronic authorization for visa-exempt travelers (expected 2025)

5. **What happens if I overstay in Germany?**
   - Fines starting at €500 (can exceed €3,000)
   - Deportation from Schengen Area
   - Entry bans: 1-5 years across entire Schengen zone
   - Recorded in Schengen Information System (SIS)
   - Severely affects future visa applications to any Schengen country
   - May face criminal charges for intentional overstays

6. **What documents do I need to enter Germany?**
   - Valid passport (3 months beyond departure from Schengen)
   - Proof of sufficient funds (minimum €45/day recommended)
   - Proof of onward travel (return/onward ticket)
   - **Travel medical insurance (minimum €30,000 coverage) - MANDATORY**
   - Proof of accommodation (hotel bookings, rental agreements, invitation letters)
   - Immigration officers may request additional documentation
